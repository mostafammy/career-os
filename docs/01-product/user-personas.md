# CareerOS User Personas

**Status:** Canonical
**Version:** 1.0

---

## Purpose

This document outlines the core user personas for CareerOS. Our design and product decisions should be evaluated against how well they solve the problems of these specific groups. They all share a common trait: they are ambitious and view their career as a continuous, strategic journey.

---

## 1. The Strategic Starter (Early-Career / Graduate)

**Profile:**
- **Age/Stage:** 20–25, completing education or entering the first few years of their career.
- **Goal:** To secure a high-leverage entry-level role, internship, or graduate program.

**The Problem:**
- Applying to dozens of roles simultaneously across various portals.
- Managing a chaotic mix of deadlines, cover letters, and company research.
- Lacks a centralized system to remember what they applied for, leading to missed deadlines or panicked interview prep.
- Often "reinvents the wheel" for every application because they haven't learned to reuse their assets.

**How CareerOS Helps:**
- **Capture:** Easily clips opportunities from LinkedIn, university portals, and company sites.
- **Organization:** Provides a single, unified pipeline of deadlines and status updates.
- **Asset Reusability:** Helps them build their first "Career Capital" database (resumes, standard essay answers) to speed up future applications.

---

## 2. The Intentional Pivot (Mid-Career Optimizer)

**Profile:**
- **Age/Stage:** 28–40, established professional looking for a strategic shift (promotion, new industry, or specialized role).
- **Goal:** Quality over quantity. They are looking for the *right* opportunity, not just *any* opportunity.

**The Problem:**
- High cost of context switching; they have a demanding day job and limited time for job searching.
- Need to tailor their narrative carefully to leverage their existing experience in a new context.
- Needs to evaluate opportunities against long-term strategic goals rather than just salary.

**How CareerOS Helps:**
- **Strategic Decision Making:** Uses decision journals and high-level strategy views to evaluate if an opportunity aligns with their 5-year goals.
- **Flow State:** Reduces the cognitive load of organizing a job hunt, letting them focus purely on preparation and execution.
- **Insight:** Helps them reflect on rejections to adjust their narrative for the next application.

---

## 3. The Serial Achiever (Fellowships, Grants & Academia)

**Profile:**
- **Age/Stage:** Varies (often researchers, academics, creatives, or non-profit leaders).
- **Goal:** Continuously secure funding, speaking engagements, scholarships, or residencies to advance their work.

**The Problem:**
- They are in a state of perpetual application.
- They have a massive repository of past proposals, personal statements, bios, and references scattered across Google Docs.
- Missing a grant deadline can set their work back by an entire year.

**How CareerOS Helps:**
- **Knowledge Compounding:** Becomes their ultimate library of reusable knowledge. They can instantly pull up what they wrote for a similar fellowship two years ago.
- **Reliability:** The OS never forgets a deadline, ensuring they don't miss out on crucial funding cycles.
- **Confidence:** They feel in complete control of their professional pipeline, reducing the anxiety of the constant hustle.

---

## Persona Alignment Checklist

When proposing a new feature, ask:
- Does this help **The Strategic Starter** manage chaos and learn to reuse assets?
- Does this help **The Intentional Pivot** make strategic decisions quickly with low cognitive load?
- Does this help **The Serial Achiever** securely store and instantly retrieve their compounding career knowledge?
