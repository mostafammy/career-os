# CareerOS Glossary

## Purpose

The CareerOS Glossary defines the canonical vocabulary of the platform.

Every term in this document has **one authoritative meaning**. Product documentation, architecture documents, database schemas, APIs, user interfaces, AI prompts, and engineering discussions should consistently use these definitions.

This glossary follows the principles of **Domain-Driven Design (DDD)** and represents the **Ubiquitous Language** of CareerOS.

**For complete entity definitions, see:** `docs/03-domain/entities.md`

---

# Guiding Principles

1. Every concept has exactly one canonical definition.
2. Similar terms should never overlap in meaning.
3. Product, Design, Engineering, and AI should use identical terminology.
4. Business terminology takes precedence over technical implementation details.
5. Definitions should describe business concepts rather than database structures.

# A

## Activity

**Definition**

An immutable record describing something that has already happened within CareerOS.

**Examples**

* Opportunity Created
* Application Submitted
* Reflection Completed
* Goal Achieved

**Related**

* Event
* Timeline
* Audit Log

**Notes**

Activities represent historical facts and cannot be modified.

---

## AI Insight

**Definition**

Knowledge generated by the Intelligence Engine to assist decision-making.

**Examples**

* Opportunity Summary
* Skill Gap Analysis
* Priority Recommendation
* Interview Preparation Advice

**Related**

* Intelligence Engine
* Recommendation
* Knowledge Entry

---

## Application

**Definition**

A concrete submission made in response to a single Opportunity.

**Examples**

* DAAD Scholarship Application
* Google STEP Internship Application
* Research Assistant Application

**Related**

* Opportunity
* Organization
* Document

**Notes**

An Opportunity may have multiple Applications.

---

# C

## Career Capital

**Definition**

The measurable collection of assets that increase long-term career opportunities.

**Categories**

* Knowledge
* Skills
* Credentials
* Portfolio
* Network
* Reputation
* Experience

**Related**

* Goal
* Mission
* Analytics

---

## CareerOS

**Definition**

A Career Operating System designed to help users discover, evaluate, execute, and learn from career opportunities throughout their professional journey.

---

## Command

**Definition**

A request expressing user intent to change the system.

**Examples**

* Capture Opportunity
* Submit Application
* Complete Reflection

**Notes**

Commands express intentions rather than completed facts.

---

# D

## Decision Journal

**Definition**

A structured record capturing the reasoning behind important career decisions.

**Purpose**

Enable future reflection and improve long-term decision quality.

---

## Document

**Definition**

A reusable asset supporting one or more applications or opportunities.

**Examples**

* CV
* Passport
* Portfolio
* Transcript
* Recommendation Letter

---

## Domain Event

**Definition**

An immutable business fact describing something that has already occurred.

**Examples**

* Application Submitted
* Goal Completed
* Reflection Finished

---

# E

## Engine

**Definition**

A logical subsystem responsible for one major business capability.

**Examples**

* Opportunity Engine
* Knowledge Engine
* Analytics Engine

**Notes**

Engines own business behavior, not user interface components.

---

## Entity

**Definition**

A uniquely identifiable business object with its own lifecycle.

**Examples**

* Opportunity
* Goal
* Organization
* Application

---

# G

## Goal

**Definition**

A measurable objective supporting one or more long-term missions.

**Examples**

* Achieve IELTS 8.0
* Secure a DAAD Scholarship
* Publish a Research Paper

**Related**

* Mission
* Milestone

---

# I

**Definition**

A temporary holding area where newly captured opportunities await classification.

**Purpose**

Separate information capture from information organization.

---

## Intelligence Engine

**Definition**

The subsystem responsible for AI-powered recommendations, summaries, predictions, and assistance.

---

# K

## Knowledge Entry

**Definition**

A reusable piece of structured knowledge derived from experience, reflection, or research.

**Examples**

* Interview Lessons
* Scholarship Tips

# M

## Milestone

**Definition**

A measurable checkpoint contributing toward the completion of a Goal.

---

## Mission

**Definition**

A strategic objective composed of multiple Goals.

**Example**

Become a Competitive International Researcher.

## Opportunity

**Definition**

A potential career advancement worth evaluating.

**Examples**

* Internship
* Scholarship
* Fellowship
* Hackathon
* Conference
* Competition
* Job
* Research Position

**Notes**

An Opportunity exists independently of whether an Application has been submitted.

## Organization

**Definition**

An external institution offering one or more Opportunities.

**Examples**

* University
* Company
* NGO
* Research Institute

## Projection

A read-optimized representation derived from one or more domain events.

A request for information that never changes system state.

# R

**Definition**

## Reminder

# T

**Definition**

A concrete action required to progress an Opportunity, Application, or Goal.

## Timeline

**Definition**

A chronological representation of significant activities, events, and milestones.

---

# U

An authenticated individual who owns a CareerOS workspace.

---

# Naming Conventions

To maintain consistency throughout CareerOS:

* Use **Opportunity**, never "Job Post" or "Listing" as the generic term.
* Use **Application**, never "Submission" when referring to the domain entity.
* Use **Organization**, not "Company", unless specifically referring to a company.
* Use **Workspace**, not "Details Page".
* Use **Knowledge Entry**, not "Note", for reusable structured knowledge.
* Use **Activity** for historical records.
* Use **Event** for immutable business facts.
* Use **Command** for user intent.
* Use **Query** for read-only operations.
* Use **Engine** for architectural modules.
* Use **Aggregate** only in architecture documentation, not in the user interface.
